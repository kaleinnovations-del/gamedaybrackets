How to make a tournament bracket

  1. Enter your number of teams (3–32).
  2. Type each team or player name in its box — or leave them as auto-numbered teams.
  3. Pick single elimination, double elimination, or round robin, then click Generate.
  4. Click a name to advance the winner, or hit Print and fill it in by hand at the event.

Single elimination vs double elimination vs round robin

Single elimination is fastest — one loss and you're out. Great for big fields and tight time slots. Double elimination gives every team a second chance through a losers bracket, so a single bad round doesn't end your day. Round robin means everyone plays everyone — nobody drives out and goes home after one game, which is why it's the favorite for backyard, social, and league play. Pick round robin when guaranteed games matter more than a fast finish.

What's the difference between this and the paid workbook?

How are byes handled?

If your team count isn't a power of two (8, 16, 32…), the top seeds automatically get first-round byes so the bracket stays balanced.
